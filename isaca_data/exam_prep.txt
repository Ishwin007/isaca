Exam Domains and Study Focus

Domain 1 — Information Systems Auditing Process (18%)
Planning, executing, and reporting IS audits using risk-based methodologies. Covers audit standards, evidence collection, and follow-up procedures.

Domain 2 — Governance and Management of IT (18%)
IT governance frameworks, strategy alignment, policy development, risk management practices, and IT performance monitoring.

Domain 3 — Information Systems Acquisition, Development and Implementation (12%)
Project management, SDLC methodologies, testing practices, change management, and release management controls.

Domain 4 — Information Systems Operations and Business Resilience (26%)
IT operations management, capacity planning, incident and problem management, business continuity planning, disaster recovery, and data backup strategies.

Domain 5 — Protection of Information Assets (26%)
Access control frameworks, network and endpoint security, cryptography, physical security, cloud security controls, and data privacy regulations.

Note: Domains 4 and 5 together account for 52% of the exam — prioritize these areas in your study plan.

Study Tips

1. Download the Exam Content Outline first and use it to build your study schedule.
2. Allocate more time to Domains 4 and 5, which carry the most exam weight.
3. Practice at least 50 QAE questions per session; focus on understanding the reasoning, not memorising answers — questions are scenario-based.
4. Take a full timed 150-question practice exam at least two weeks before your test date.
5. Join a local ISACA chapter or online study group for accountability and peer discussion.
6. Plan for 3 to 6 months of preparation time depending on your existing experience.

Exam Format

• Format: Computer-based test (CBT) at PSI testing centres or via remote proctoring
• Number of Questions: 150 multiple-choice
• Time Allowed: 4 hours
• Passing Score: 450 out of 800
• Languages: English, Chinese (Simplified), Spanish, French, German, Hebrew, Italian, Japanese, Korean, and Turkish

Maintaining Your CISA Certification (CPE Requirements)

After earning CISA, you must earn and report Continuing Professional Education hours to keep the credential active:
• Minimum 20 CPE hours per calendar year
• Minimum 120 CPE hours per 3-year certification period
• Annual maintenance fee applies
• Failure to meet CPE requirements results in certification revocation
