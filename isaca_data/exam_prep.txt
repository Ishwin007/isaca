CISA Exam Preparation Guide
Source: https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cisa

This guide summarizes the key resources, study tips, and exam details to help you prepare for the CISA exam.

Official Study Materials from ISACA

1. CISA Review Manual
A comprehensive study guide covering all five exam domains in detail. Updated regularly to stay aligned with the current exam syllabus. Available in print and digital formats.

2. Questions, Answers and Explanations (QAE) Database
A bank of over 1,000 practice questions with detailed explanations for each answer. Organized by domain so you can focus on areas where you need more practice. Available through ISACA's online learning platform.
Price: Member US$149 / Non-Member US$199

3. Online Review Course
A self-paced online course covering all five domains through video lessons, exercises, and quizzes. Earns CPE credits on completion.
Price: Member US$499 / Non-Member US$649

4. Practice Exam
A full 150-question timed mock exam that simulates the real test experience. Includes a domain-level performance report to identify weak areas.
Price: Member US$99 / Non-Member US$139

Exam Domain Breakdown

Domain 1 — Information Systems Auditing Process (18%)
Covers how to plan, conduct, and report IT audits using risk-based approaches. Includes audit standards, gathering evidence, and following up on findings.

Domain 2 — Governance and Management of IT (18%)
Covers IT governance frameworks, how IT strategy aligns with business goals, policy development, risk management, and performance monitoring.

Domain 3 — Information Systems Acquisition, Development and Implementation (12%)
Covers project management, software development lifecycles, testing practices, change management, and release controls.

Domain 4 — Information Systems Operations and Business Resilience (26%)
Covers IT operations, capacity planning, incident management, business continuity, disaster recovery, and data backup strategies.

Domain 5 — Protection of Information Assets (26%)
Covers access control, network and endpoint security, cryptography, physical security, cloud security, and data privacy requirements.

Study Tip: Domains 4 and 5 together make up 52% of the exam. Spend more time on these two areas.

Study Tips

1. Start by downloading the Exam Content Outline from isaca.org and build your study schedule around it.
2. Spend the most time on Domains 4 and 5 since they carry the most exam weight combined.
3. Practice with QAE questions regularly — focus on understanding why an answer is correct, not just memorizing it. The exam uses scenario-based questions.
4. Take a full timed practice exam at least two weeks before your exam date to identify gaps.
5. Join a local ISACA chapter or online study group to stay motivated and learn from others.
6. Allow 3 to 6 months for preparation depending on your current experience level.

Exam Format

•	Format: Computer-based at PSI testing centers worldwide or via online remote proctoring
•	Number of Questions: 150 multiple-choice questions
•	Time Allowed: 4 hours
•	Passing Score: 450 out of 800
•	Available Languages: English, Chinese (Simplified), Spanish, French, German, Hebrew, Italian, Japanese, Korean, and Turkish

Maintaining CISA After You Earn It

Once certified, you must keep your CISA active by earning Continuing Professional Education (CPE) hours:
•	At least 20 CPE hours per calendar year
•	At least 120 CPE hours over each 3-year certification period
•	Annual maintenance fee applies
•	Not meeting CPE requirements can result in certification suspension or revocation
