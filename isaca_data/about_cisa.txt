CISA — Certified Information Systems Auditor
Source: https://www.isaca.org/credentialing/cisa

What is CISA?
CISA is one of the most widely recognized certifications for professionals who audit, monitor, and assess IT systems and business processes. It is designed for individuals who want to demonstrate their ability to evaluate an organization's IT controls, identify risks, and ensure systems are managed securely and effectively.

The certification covers both traditional IT audit practices and emerging technologies such as AI and blockchain, making it relevant for modern audit professionals.

Exam Domains
The CISA exam covers five areas:
•	Information Systems Auditing Process
•	Governance and Management of Information Technology
•	Information Systems Acquisition, Development and Implementation
•	Information Systems Operations and Business Resilience
•	Protection of Information Assets

Certification Requirements
To become CISA certified, candidates must:
•	Pass the CISA exam
•	Pay a US$50 application processing fee
•	Submit proof of at least 5 years of relevant work experience
•	Agree to ISACA's Code of Professional Ethics
•	Follow ISACA's Continuing Professional Education (CPE) Policy
•	Comply with Information Systems Auditing Standards

Candidates have up to five years from the date they pass the exam to submit their certification application.

Exam Fees
•	ISACA Member: US$575
•	Non-Member: US$760

How to Register
The CISA exam is computer-based and can be taken at PSI testing centers around the world or online via remote proctoring. There are no application deadlines — registration is open year-round.

Steps to register:
1.	Log in to your ISACA account at www.isaca.org
2.	Go to Certification and CPE Management
3.	Select Schedule Your Exam
4.	Complete scheduling through the PSI portal

Eligibility Window
After registering and paying, candidates have 6 months to sit the exam. Exam slots can be booked up to 90 days in advance. If you need to reschedule, you can do so without a penalty as long as you give at least 48 hours notice.

Accreditation
CISA holds ANAB (ANSI National Accreditation Board) accreditation, which means it meets internationally recognized standards for professional certifications.
