ISACA Chapters and Local Community
Source: https://www.isaca.org/membership/local-chapters

ISACA has 231 chapters across 6 global regions, giving members access to a professional community right in their own city or country. Chapters are the local heartbeat of ISACA — where networking, learning, and career growth happen face to face.

Global Reach
•	231 chapters worldwide
•	Regions covered: Africa, Asia, Europe, Latin America, North America, Oceania
•	Chapters exist in over 100 countries

What Do Chapters Do?
Local chapters organize regular activities including:
•	Monthly or quarterly chapter meetings with guest speakers
•	Local training sessions and workshops
•	Study groups for ISACA certification exams
•	Networking events connecting members with employers and peers
•	Annual chapter conferences
•	Volunteer and leadership opportunities
•	CPE-eligible events — attending chapter events earns CPE hours toward certification maintenance

Benefits of Joining Your Local Chapter
•	Network with professionals in your city working in IT audit, security, risk, and governance
•	Find a mentor or become one through chapter mentoring programs
•	Earn CPE credits at local events (typically 1-3 CPE hours per event)
•	Access job leads and career opportunities shared within the chapter
•	Get involved in leadership roles — chapter president, secretary, education director, and more
•	Stay current with local regulatory and industry developments
•	Represent ISACA in your community

How to Find Your Local Chapter
1.	Visit www.isaca.org/membership/local-chapters
2.	Use the chapter directory to search by region, country, or city
3.	Each chapter listing includes contact details, territory coverage, and upcoming events
4.	Reach out to the chapter directly or register for an upcoming event

Chapter Membership
•	ISACA members are required to join a local chapter if one exists in their area
•	Chapter membership is typically included in your ISACA membership fee
•	Some chapters charge a small additional local fee

Volunteering with Your Chapter
Chapters rely on volunteers to thrive. Opportunities include:
•	Serving on the chapter board (president, VP, treasurer, secretary)
•	Leading education and training initiatives
•	Organizing events and speaker programs
•	Mentoring newer members
•	Representing ISACA at local universities and industry events

Starting a New Chapter
If no chapter exists in your area, ISACA supports professionals in establishing new chapters. Contact ISACA directly at www.isaca.org for guidance on the chapter formation process.

ISACA Chapter Awards
Outstanding chapters and volunteers are recognized annually through ISACA's Chapter Awards program, celebrating excellence in community building, education, and member engagement.
