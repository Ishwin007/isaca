CERTIFICATION SALARIES AND JOB MARKET DATA
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ISACA certifications are consistently ranked among the highest-paying IT credentials in the world. Below is a summary of salary data and job market demand for each certification.

CISA — Certified Information Systems Auditor
• Average Annual Salary: US$149,000+
• Job Titles: IT Auditor, IS Auditor, Audit Manager, Compliance Analyst, Risk Analyst
• On-the-Job Improvement Reported: 70% of holders
• Pay Boost After Certification: 22% of holders received a raise
• Demand: High — required by many government agencies, banks, and enterprises globally

CISM — Certified Information Security Manager
• Average Annual Salary: US$149,000+
• Job Titles: Information Security Manager, CISO, Security Director, IT Security Consultant
• On-the-Job Improvement Reported: 73% of holders
• Pay Boost After Certification: 26% of holders received a raise
• Demand: Very High — one of the most sought-after management-level security credentials

CRISC — Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control
• Average Annual Salary: US$151,000+
• Job Titles: IT Risk Manager, Risk Analyst, GRC Analyst, Compliance Officer, Risk Consultant
• On-the-Job Improvement Reported: 71% of holders
• Pay Boost After Certification: 25% of holders received a raise
• Demand: Very High — especially in banking, finance, insurance, and regulated industries

CDPSE — Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer
• Average Annual Salary: US$150,000+
• Job Titles: Privacy Engineer, Data Protection Officer, Privacy Architect, Privacy Analyst
• Demand: Rapidly growing — driven by GDPR, CCPA, and expanding global privacy regulations

CGEIT — Certified in the Governance of Enterprise IT
• Average Annual Salary: US$130,000+
• Job Titles: IT Director, CIO, Enterprise Architect, IT Governance Manager, Senior IT Consultant
• Demand: Moderate-High — valued by senior leadership and board-level advisory roles

CCOA — Certified Cybersecurity Operations Analyst
• Average Annual Salary: Emerging — certification launched in 2025
• Job Titles: SOC Analyst, Cybersecurity Analyst, Incident Response Analyst, Threat Analyst
• Demand: Very High — cybersecurity operations roles are among the fastest growing in tech
• Exam Cost: Lower than other ISACA certs (US$399 member) — great entry point

AAIA — Advanced in AI Audit
• Average Annual Salary: Premium over base CISA/CIA salary expected
• Job Titles: AI Auditor, AI Governance Specialist, Technology Risk Advisor
• Demand: Rapidly emerging — organizations are scrambling to audit AI systems
• Requires: Active CISA or equivalent audit qualification

AAIR — Advanced in AI Risk
• Average Annual Salary: Premium over base CRISC/risk salary expected
• Job Titles: AI Risk Manager, AI Governance Analyst, Technology Risk Director
• Demand: Rapidly emerging — AI risk is one of the top concerns for boards globally
• Requires: Active CRISC, CISM, CISA, or equivalent

AAISM — Advanced in AI Security Management
• Average Annual Salary: Premium over base CISM salary expected
• Job Titles: AI Security Manager, AI Security Director, CISO (AI focus)
• Demand: Rapidly emerging — protecting AI systems is a new frontier in security
• Requires: Active CISM or CISSP

General Market Trends
• ISACA reports a 90%+ renewal rate across all certifications — indicating strong career value
• Members save 25% on exam fees — making membership worthwhile if pursuing any certification
• 300,000+ ISACA certifications awarded across 186 countries
• AI-related certifications (AAIA, AAIR, AAISM) are expected to see significant salary premiums as AI governance becomes a regulatory requirement globally
• The cybersecurity talent gap continues to grow — ISACA's State of Cybersecurity report consistently shows demand outpacing supply

Note: Salary figures are approximate averages based on ISACA's published data. Actual salaries vary by country, industry, experience level, and organization size.

BY FOCUS AREA

Audit & Assurance
• CISA — the gold standard for IT auditors. Covers audit process, governance, operations, and asset protection.

Security
• CISM — management-level. Focuses on building and governing security programs.
• CCOA — technical, hands-on. Focuses on SOC work, threat detection, and incident response.
• AAISM — advanced AI security. Builds on CISM with AI-specific security management.

Risk Management
• CRISC — the leading IT risk certification. Bridges IT and business risk.
• AAIR — advanced AI risk. Builds on CRISC with AI lifecycle risk management.

Governance
• CGEIT — enterprise IT governance at the senior/board level.

Privacy
• CDPSE — technical implementation of privacy into systems.

AI (Advanced)
• AAIA — auditing AI systems (requires CISA)
• AAIR — managing AI risk (requires CRISC or equivalent)
• AAISM — securing AI (requires CISM or CISSP)

BY CAREER LEVEL

Early Career (0-3 years experience)
• CCOA — no experience requirement, hands-on cyber focus, lower exam cost
• Start studying for CISA, CISM, or CRISC while building work experience

Mid Career (3-7 years experience)
• CISA — if you work in audit, compliance, or assurance
• CISM — if you work in security management or want to move into it
• CRISC — if you work in IT risk, GRC, or compliance
• CDPSE — if you work in privacy, data protection, or engineering

Senior Career (7+ years experience)
• CGEIT — if you are in or moving toward IT leadership or board advisory
• AAIA — if you are a senior auditor wanting AI specialization
• AAIR — if you are a senior risk professional wanting AI specialization
• AAISM — if you are a security leader wanting AI security expertise

BY INDUSTRY

Technology Companies: CISM, CDPSE, CCOA, AAISM
Government & Public Sector: CISA, CRISC, CGEIT

WHICH ISACA CERTIFICATION IS RIGHT FOR ME?
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Use this guide to find the best ISACA certification based on your current role, career goals, and experience level.

STEP 1 — WHAT IS YOUR CURRENT ROLE?

I work in IT Audit or Internal Audit
→ Start with CISA. It is the global standard for IT auditors and the most recognized audit credential in the world.
→ After CISA: Consider AAIA (AI Audit) to specialize in auditing artificial intelligence systems.

I work in Information Security or Cybersecurity
→ If you are in a management or leadership role: Start with CISM.
→ If you are a hands-on analyst working in a SOC: Start with CCOA.
→ After CISM: Consider AAISM (AI Security Management) for AI-specific expertise.

I work in IT Risk Management or GRC (Governance, Risk, Compliance)
→ Start with CRISC. It is the leading certification for IT risk professionals.
→ After CRISC: Consider AAIR (AI Risk) to specialize in managing AI-related risks.

I work in IT Governance or Senior IT Leadership (CIO, IT Director)
→ Start with CGEIT. It is designed for professionals governing enterprise IT at a strategic level.
→ Consider CISA or CISM first if you don't already hold one.

I work in Data Privacy or Data Protection
→ Start with CDPSE. It is the only technical privacy certification focused on implementing privacy by design.
→ Pairs well with CISA or CRISC.

I am new to cybersecurity or just starting out
→ Start with CCOA. It has no experience requirement, a lower exam cost, and focuses on practical, hands-on skills used daily in cybersecurity roles.

I want to specialize in Artificial Intelligence
→ If you are an auditor: Earn CISA first, then pursue AAIA.
→ If you are in risk: Earn CRISC first, then pursue AAIR.
→ If you are in security management: Earn CISM first, then pursue AAISM.

STEP 2 — WHAT IS YOUR EXPERIENCE LEVEL?

0-2 Years of Experience
→ Best option: CCOA (no experience requirement)
→ Start building experience toward CISA, CISM, or CRISC
→ Consider student ISACA membership while studying

3-5 Years of Experience
→ CISA — if in audit, compliance, or assurance
→ CISM — if in security or moving into security management
→ CRISC — if in risk, GRC, or compliance
→ CDPSE — if in privacy, data protection, or technical roles

5+ Years of Experience
→ CGEIT — for senior IT leaders and governance professionals
→ AAIA, AAIR, or AAISM — for specialists wanting advanced AI credentials

STEP 3 — WHAT IS YOUR GOAL?

I want a pay raise
→ CRISC holders report the highest average salaries (US$151K+)
→ CISM and CISA are close behind (US$149K+)
→ All three are proven to result in pay increases for a significant portion of holders

I want to move into management
→ CISM is the clearest path to security management and CISO roles
→ CGEIT for IT governance and board-level advisory

I want to be recognized globally
→ CISA is the most globally recognized ISACA certification — accepted in 186 countries
→ CISM is a close second for global recognition

I want to specialize in a growing area
→ AI certifications (AAIA, AAIR, AAISM) — fastest growing demand
→ CDPSE — growing demand driven by global privacy regulations
→ CCOA — cybersecurity operations roles are among the fastest growing in tech

PRACTICAL TIPS

1. Become an ISACA member first — saves US$185 per exam for CISA/CISM/CRISC/CGEIT/CDPSE
2. Use ISACA's official review manuals and question banks — they are the most aligned with the exam
3. Join your local ISACA chapter — study groups, networking, and CPE opportunities
4. You do not need to pass the exam before applying for membership — join first, save on the exam
5. Check the work experience requirements for your chosen certification before registering

For personalized guidance, visit www.isaca.org/credentialing or contact your local ISACA chapter.
