ISACA Certifications — Complete Guide
Source: https://www.isaca.org/credentialing

ISACA has awarded 300,000+ certifications across 186 countries. Credentialing isn't a professional luxury — it's a career necessity. ISACA certifications validate your expertise, boost your earning potential, and are recognized by employers worldwide.

Active Professional Certifications

1. CISA — Certified Information Systems Auditor
   The global standard for IT audit, assurance, and control professionals.

2. CISM — Certified Information Security Manager
   For professionals who manage, design, and oversee enterprise information security programs.

3. CRISC — Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control
   For IT risk management professionals who bridge IT and business risk.

4. CGEIT — Certified in the Governance of Enterprise IT
   For professionals who manage or advise on enterprise IT governance.

5. CDPSE — Certified Data Privacy Solutions Engineer
   For technical professionals who implement privacy by design in systems and applications.

6. CCOA — Certified Cybersecurity Operations Analyst
   For cybersecurity analysts working in security operations centers (SOC).

7. AAIA — Advanced in AI Audit
   For auditors specializing in auditing artificial intelligence systems and processes.

8. AAIR — Advanced in AI Risk
   For risk professionals focused on managing risks associated with AI technologies.

9. AAISM — Advanced in AI Security Management
   For security managers overseeing AI security programs and frameworks.

10. CCP — CMMC Certified Professional
    For professionals supporting CMMC (Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification) compliance.

11. CCA — CMMC Certified Assessor
    For individuals authorized to conduct CMMC assessments.

12. LCCA — Lead CMMC Certified Assessor Designation
    Advanced designation for experienced CMMC lead assessors.

13. CCI — CMMC Credentialed Instructor
    For instructors delivering authorized CMMC training.

How to Get Certified — General Steps
1.	Choose the certification that aligns with your career goals
2.	Create or log in to your ISACA account at www.isaca.org
3.	Register and pay for the exam (members receive a 25% discount)
4.	Study using ISACA's official review manuals and question banks
5.	Schedule your exam through the PSI testing portal (available online or at test centers)
6.	Pass the exam within your six-month eligibility window
7.	Submit your work experience application and pay the US$50 processing fee
8.	Maintain your certification by earning annual CPE hours

Key Facts
