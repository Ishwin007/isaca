Which ISACA Certification is Right for Me?
Source: https://www.isaca.org/credentialing

Use this guide to find the best ISACA certification based on your current role, career goals, and experience level.

STEP 1 — WHAT IS YOUR CURRENT ROLE?

I work in IT Audit or Internal Audit
→ Start with CISA. It is the global standard for IT auditors and the most recognized audit credential in the world.
→ After CISA: Consider AAIA (AI Audit) to specialize in auditing artificial intelligence systems.

I work in Information Security or Cybersecurity
→ If you are in a management or leadership role: Start with CISM.
→ If you are a hands-on analyst working in a SOC: Start with CCOA.
→ After CISM: Consider AAISM (AI Security Management) for AI-specific expertise.

I work in IT Risk Management or GRC (Governance, Risk, Compliance)
→ Start with CRISC. It is the leading certification for IT risk professionals.
→ After CRISC: Consider AAIR (AI Risk) to specialize in managing AI-related risks.

I work in IT Governance or Senior IT Leadership (CIO, IT Director)
→ Start with CGEIT. It is designed for professionals governing enterprise IT at a strategic level.
→ Consider CISA or CISM first if you don't already hold one.

I work in Data Privacy or Data Protection
→ Start with CDPSE. It is the only technical privacy certification focused on implementing privacy by design.
→ Pairs well with CISA or CRISC.

I am new to cybersecurity or just starting out
→ Start with CCOA. It has no experience requirement, a lower exam cost, and focuses on practical, hands-on skills used daily in cybersecurity roles.

I want to specialize in Artificial Intelligence
→ If you are an auditor: Earn CISA first, then pursue AAIA.
→ If you are in risk: Earn CRISC first, then pursue AAIR.
→ If you are in security management: Earn CISM first, then pursue AAISM.

---

STEP 2 — WHAT IS YOUR EXPERIENCE LEVEL?

0-2 Years of Experience
→ Best option: CCOA (no experience requirement)
→ Start building experience toward CISA, CISM, or CRISC
→ Consider student ISACA membership while studying

3-5 Years of Experience
→ CISA — if in audit, compliance, or assurance
→ CISM — if in security or moving into security management
→ CRISC — if in risk, GRC, or compliance
→ CDPSE — if in privacy, data protection, or technical roles

5+ Years of Experience
→ CGEIT — for senior IT leaders and governance professionals
→ AAIA, AAIR, or AAISM — for specialists wanting advanced AI credentials

---

STEP 3 — WHAT IS YOUR GOAL?

I want a pay raise
→ CRISC holders report the highest average salaries (US$151K+)
→ CISM and CISA are close behind (US$149K+)
→ All three are proven to result in pay increases for a significant portion of holders

I want to move into management
→ CISM is the clearest path to security management and CISO roles
→ CGEIT for IT governance and board-level advisory

I want to be recognized globally
→ CISA is the most globally recognized ISACA certification — accepted in 186 countries
→ CISM is a close second for global recognition

I want to specialize in a growing area
→ AI certifications (AAIA, AAIR, AAISM) — fastest growing demand
→ CDPSE — growing demand driven by global privacy regulations
→ CCOA — cybersecurity operations roles are among the fastest growing in tech

I want to audit AI systems
→ Earn CISA first → then pursue AAIA

I want to manage AI risks
→ Earn CRISC first → then pursue AAIR

PRACTICAL TIPS

1. Become an ISACA member first — saves US$185 per exam for CISA/CISM/CRISC/CGEIT/CDPSE
2. Use ISACA's official review manuals and question banks — they are the most aligned with the exam
3. Join your local ISACA chapter — study groups, networking, and CPE opportunities
4. You do not need to pass the exam before applying for membership — join first, save on the exam
5. Check the work experience requirements for your chosen certification before registering

For personalized guidance, visit www.isaca.org/credentialing or contact your local ISACA chapter.
